🇬🇧 UNITED KINGDOM (GB)
------------------------
TAX YEAR: 6 April - 5 April
CURRENCY: GBP (£)

PERSONAL TAX 2025-26:
- Personal Allowance: £12,570 (tapers £1 per £2 over £100k, zero at £125,140)
- Basic Rate: 20% (£12,571-£50,270)
- Higher Rate: 40% (£50,271-£125,140)
- Additional Rate: 45% (over £125,140)
- National Insurance: 8% (basic), 2% (higher)

CAPITAL GAINS TAX:
- Basic Rate: 14% (from April 2025)
- Higher Rate: 24% (from April 2025)
- Annual Exempt Amount: £3,000
- Reporting threshold: £50,000 proceeds

DIVIDEND TAX:
- Basic: 8.75%
- Higher: 33.75%
- Additional: 39.35%
- Allowance: £500 (from April 2024)

CORPORATION TAX:
- Small Profits Rate: 19% (profits ≤£50,000)
- Main Rate: 25% (profits >£250,000)
- Marginal Relief: Between £50k-£250k

VAT:
- Standard: 20%
- Reduced: 5%
- Zero: 0%
- Registration: £85,000 turnover

PENSION:
- State Pension Age: 66 (rising to 67 by 2028, 68 by 2046)
- Private Access: 57 (rising to 58)
- Annual Allowance: £60,000
- Tax Relief: Marginal rate
- Auto-enrolment: 5% employee, 3% employer minimum

ISA:
- Annual Allowance: £20,000
- Types: Cash, Stocks & Shares, Lifetime, Junior, Innovative Finance
- LISA: £4,000 + 25% bonus, max age 40 to open

DIGITAL NOMAD VISA: No
RETIREMENT VISA: No

DOUBLE TAX TREATIES: 130+ countries including all major economies

NOTES:
- Scotland has devolved income tax rates
- Wales has limited devolved powers
- Non-dom regime abolished from April 2025 (replaced by FIG regime)
- Inheritance Tax: 40% over £325,000 nil-rate band


🇵🇹 PORTUGAL (PT)
------------------
TAX YEAR: 1 January - 31 December
CURRENCY: EUR (€)

PERSONAL TAX 2025:
- Minimum: €4,104
- Scale: 14.5% to 48% (9 brackets)
- Brackets: €0-€7,479 (14.5%), €7,480-€11,284 (21%), up to €78,834+ (48%)
- Solidarity Surcharge: 2.5%-5% for high incomes

CAPITAL GAINS TAX:
- Rate: 28% flat OR marginal income tax scale (whichever lower for residents)
- Exemption: None (but inflation adjustment available)
- Crypto: 28% (new rules 2023)

CORPORATION TAX:
- Standard: 21%
- Reduced: 17% (first €50,000 for SMEs)
- Madeira: 14.7%
- Azores: 16.8%

VAT (IVA):
- Standard: 23%
- Intermediate: 13%
- Reduced: 6%
- Madeira: 22%/12%/5%
- Azores: 18%/9%/4%

PENSION:
- State Pension Age: 66
- Private Access: 55 (early retirement penalties apply)
- Contribution Limit: €4,000-€12,000 (varies by age)
- Tax Relief: Yes, at marginal rate

NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) Regime:
- Duration: 10 years
- Foreign-sourced income: Exempt if taxed in source country OR 0% if not
- Portuguese-sourced "high value" employment: 20% flat
- Pension income: 10% flat
- Eligibility: Not been tax resident in Portugal previous 5 years

DIGITAL NOMAD VISA (D8):
- Launch: October 2022
- Duration: 2 years (renewable)
- Max: 5 years total
- Min Income: €3,280/month (€39,360/year)
- Path to Residency: Yes
- Tax Benefits: NHR available after visa

GOLDEN VISA:
- Status: Suspended for real estate (October 2023)
- Alternative: Investment funds, cultural heritage, research, business
- Min Investment: €500,000

NOTES:
- 183-day rule for tax residency
- Centre of vital interests test if close to 183 days
- Wealth tax: None (abolished 2003)
- Inheritance Tax: 0% for direct family (spouse, children)
- Property Transfer Tax: 0-6% (IMT)


🇧🇪 BELGIUM (BE)
-----------------
TAX YEAR: 1 January - 31 December
CURRENCY: EUR (€)

PERSONAL TAX 2025:
- Scale: 25% to 50% (4 brackets plus local taxes)
- Regional variation: Additional regional tax (Brussels 7%, Flanders 0-9%, Wallonia 0-9%)
- Social Security: 13.07% employee contribution

CAPITAL GAINS TAX:
- Private assets: 0% (Belgium has NO CGT on private assets)
- Exception: Speculative transactions (30% if within 6 months)

CORPORATION TAX:
- Standard: 25%
- Reduced: 20% (first €100,000 for SMEs)

VAT (TVA/BTW):
- Standard: 21%
- Reduced: 12% (restaurants, energy), 6% (essential goods)

PENSION:
- State Pension Age: 65 (rising to 67 from 2025)
- Private Access: 60 (early penalties)
- Contribution: Tax relief at 30%
- Limits: €1,000-€2,500 depending on employment status

DIGITAL NOMAD VISA: No (but Professional Card available for self-employed)

NOTES:
- Wealth tax on securities accounts: 0.15% over €1 million
- Inheritance tax: varies by region (Brussels 3-65%, Flanders 3-55%)
- No general wealth tax
- Stock exchange tax: 0.12-1.32% on transactions


🇪🇸 SPAIN (ES)
---------------
TAX YEAR: 1 January - 31 December
CURRENCY: EUR (€)

PERSONAL TAX 2025:
- State: 9.5% to 22.5% (6 brackets)
- Regional: Additional 0-18% (varies by autonomous community)
- Total effective: 19% to 47%
- Madrid: Lowest rates (special tax benefits)
- Catalonia: Higher rates

CAPITAL GAINS TAX:
- Up to €6,000: 19%
- €6,000-€50,000: 21%
- €50,000-€200,000: 23%
- Over €200,000: 26%
- Exemption: None (but inflation adjustment for pre-2006 assets)

CORPORATION TAX:
- Standard: 25%
- Reduced: 15% (first €300,000 for new companies)
- Navarra: 24%
- Basque Country: 24%

VAT (IVA):
- Standard: 21%
- Reduced: 10% (food, restaurants, housing)
- Super-reduced: 4% (essential food, books, medicine)

PENSION:
- State Pension Age: 66 (rising to 67)
- Early Retirement: 63-65 (with penalties)
- Contribution Limit: €1,500-€8,000
- Tax Relief: Yes

DIGITAL NOMAD VISA:
- Launch: 2023
- Duration: 1 year (renewable up to 5 years)
- Min Income: €2,160/month (200% of minimum wage)
- Requirements: Remote work for non-Spanish company, health insurance, clean criminal record
- Path to Residency: Yes
- Tax Benefits: Beckham Law option (first 6 years taxed as non-resident)

BECKHAM LAW:
- For new residents moving to Spain

- Foreign income: Exempt
- Duration: 6 years
- Must not have been resident in Spain previous 5 years

NOTES:
- Wealth Tax: 0.2-3.5% on worldwide assets over €700,000 (varies by region)
- Madrid: 100% wealth tax rebate
- Inheritance Tax: Varies by region (some 99% rebates)
- 183-day rule for residency
- Exit tax: 19% unrealized gains if leaving after 10 years

🇫🇷 FRANCE (FR)
----------------
TAX YEAR: 1 January - 31 December
CURRENCY: EUR (€)

PERSONAL TAX 2025:
- Scale: 0% to 45% (5 brackets)
- Brackets: €0-€11,294 (0%), €11,295-€28,797 (11%), up to €177,106+ (45%)
- CSG/CRDS: 9.7% social charges on income
- Total effective: Higher than headline rates due to social charges

CAPITAL GAINS TAX:
- Flat Tax (PFU): 30% (12.8% tax + 17.2% social charges)
- OR marginal income tax scale (choose better option)
- Allowance: None
- Property: Progressive reduction (6% per year after 6 years, full exempt after 22 years)

CORPORATION TAX:
- Standard: 25%
- Reduced: 15% (first €42,500 for SMEs)

VAT (TVA):

EXAMPLE 1: UK RESIDENT WITH NIGERIAN BUSINESS
-----------------------------------------------

USER PROFILE:
- Primary Residence: United Kingdom (tax resident)
- Secondary Residence 1: Nigeria
- Days in Nigeria: 45 days/year
- Citizenship: United Kingdom, Nigeria (dual)
- Employment: Business Owner
- Primary Income: Nigerian business (₦8,000,000/year)
- UK Income: £30,000 agency work

ASSETS:
- Lagos Manufacturing Unit (Nigeria)
  - Value: ₦50,000,000
  - Income: ₦8,000,000/year
  - Local tax paid: ₦2,400,000

TAX CALCULATION:

UK Tax:
- Worldwide income: £30,000 + £14,500 (converted) = £44,500
- Tax due: (£44,500 - £12,570) × 20% = £6,386
- Foreign tax credit: ₦2,400,000 = £2,640
- Credit limit: £14,500/£44,500 × £6,386 = £2,080
- Allowed credit: £2,080 (lower of actual and limit)
- UK tax paid (PAYE): £3,486
- Net UK tax: £6,386 - £2,080 - £3,486 = £820

Nigeria Tax:
- Local tax: ₦2,400,000 (already paid)
- No further liability

Result: User pays £820 to UK, ₦2,400,000 to Nigeria. Total effective rate ~15%.
