5.1 KEY RISKS BY PHASE
======================

PHASE 2/3 RISKS (Entry-Level Equipment):
-----------------------------------------
Risk: Insufficient demand for scanning/printing services
Mitigation:
• Pre-sell services before purchasing
• Start with service bureau model (print for others)
• Build client base before scaling
• Diversify into multiple verticals

Risk: Equipment obsolescence
Mitigation:
• Focus on established brands (Formlabs, Creality)
• Avoid cutting-edge unproven technology
• Plan 3-5 year replacement cycles

Risk: Skills gap
Mitigation:
• Extensive training before launch
• Hire experienced operator if needed
• Start simple, increase complexity gradually


PHASE 4 RISKS (Metal 3D Printing):
-----------------------------------
Risk: £100K+ equipment underutilized
Mitigation:
• Secure 2-3 anchor clients before purchase
• Offer services on existing Form 4L first
• Build proven track record
• Conservative revenue projections

Risk: Technical complexity (sintering failures)
Mitigation:
• Comprehensive training from Desktop Metal
• Start with stainless steel (easier than titanium)
• Build quality control processes
• Accept learning curve (budget for scrap parts)

Risk: Certification requirements
Mitigation:
• Begin ISO 9001 process early
• Consult with certification body
• Budget £5,000-10,000 for certification
• Hire quality manager


PHASE 5+ RISKS (DMLS Industrial):
----------------------------------
Risk: £700K+ capital at stake
Mitigation:
• 3-5 years proven business history
• Strong financial reserves (£200K+)
• Diversified client base (no single client >30%)
• Conservative financing (don't over-leverage)

Risk: High fixed costs (£60K-120K/month)
Mitigation:
• Secured long-term contracts before scaling
• Maintain 6-12 month cash reserves
• Conservative growth (don't hire too fast)
• Diversify revenue streams

Risk: Certification failures (ISO, aerospace)
Mitigation:
• Hire experienced quality manager
• Engage consultant early (£10K-20K)
• Budget 6-12 months for certification process
• Start with simpler certifications first
