CREDIT UNION GUIDE - AN ALTERNATIVE FINANCIAL OPTION
======================================================

WHAT IS A CREDIT UNION?
=======================

A credit union is a not-for-profit financial co-operative, owned and run by its members,
for the benefit of its members.

Key Difference from Banks:
- Banks are for-profit (aim to maximise returns for shareholders)
- Credit unions are not-for-profit (aim to serve their community)

OWNERSHIP & STRUCTURE:
- Owned by members (you become a member-owner when you join)
- Each member gets one vote (regardless of account size)
- Surplus profits are returned to members as dividends or better rates
- Governed by a volunteer board elected by members

ELIGIBILITY ("COMMON BOND"):
To join a credit union, you must share a "common bond" with other members:
- Live in the same area (postcode-based)
- Work for the same employer (company-based credit unions)
- Belong to the same trade union or professional association
- Attend the same church or place of worship
- Part of the same community or housing association

Example: "ABC Credit Union serves anyone who lives or works in Manchester"


CREDIT UNION VS TRADITIONAL BANK
=================================

Feature                     | Credit Union          | Traditional Bank
----------------------------|-----------------------|-------------------
Purpose                     | Not-for-profit        | For-profit
Ownership                   | Member-owned          | Shareholder-owned
Eligibility                 | Common bond required  | Open to all UK residents
Savings rates               | Often competitive     | Usually low
Loan rates                  | Capped at 42.6% APR   | Varies widely
Credit checking             | More holistic/flexible| Strict scoring
Personal service            | Community-focused     | Often automated
FSCS Protection             | £85,000 guaranteed    | £85,000 guaranteed


HOW CREDIT UNIONS CAN HELP YOUR SITUATION
==========================================

1. DEBT CONSOLIDATION (Potential Strategy)
-----------------------------------------
Current Situation:
- Resolve: £160 balance, £30/month
- HMV: £700 balance, £30/month  
- Jason: £800 balance, £100/month
- Total debt: £1,660
- Total monthly minimums: £160

Credit Union Loan Option:
- Borrow £1,660 from credit union
- Pay off all three debts immediately
- Single monthly payment to credit union
- APR capped at 42.6% (often lower in practice)
- Typical rates: 12.7% to 42.6% APR depending on circumstances

Benefits:
- Simplifies finances (one payment vs three)
- May lower total interest paid
- Builds relationship with community lender
- Improves credit score as old debts paid

Considerations:
- Need to qualify for loan (common bond + affordability check)
- May need to open savings account first (often £5-10)
- Early repayment may have small penalty
- Credit check will be performed

2. EMERGENCY FUND SAVINGS
-------------------------
Where to Keep Emergency Fund:
Option A: Coinbase GBP Savings (3.75% AER) - Current plan
Option B: Credit Union Savings Account

Credit Union Savings Benefits:
- FSCS protected up to £85,000 (same as banks)
- Some offer competitive interest rates
- Dividends paid annually from profits
- Supporting local community
- Some offer "Save as You Borrow" (mandatory savings with loans)

Typical Rates:
- 0.5% to 3% (varies by credit union)
- Dividends (profit share) can add 1-3% on top
- Often better than high street banks

3. FUTURE BORROWING NEEDS
--------------------------
When You Might Need Credit:
- Phase 6: Business funding (after debt cleared)
- Home improvements before selling/renting
- Car replacement
- Unexpected major expense

Credit Union Advantages for Future:
- Already established relationship
- Known payment history with them
- More likely to approve if you've saved regularly
- Ethical lending practices
- Free life insurance often included with loans


FINDING A CREDIT UNION
======================

Search Methods:
1. Find Your Credit Union website: findyourcreditunion.co.uk
   - Search by postcode
   - Shows eligible credit unions in your area

2. ABCUL (Association of British Credit Unions): abcul.org
   - National database
   - Information on credit union movement

3. Ask your employer
   - Many companies have workplace credit unions
   - Check HR or employee benefits

4. Check trade union membership
   - Unions often have affiliated credit unions

5. Local community centres
   - Some housing associations run credit unions
   - Churches and faith groups


HOW TO JOIN (Step-by-Step)
===========================

Step 1: Find Eligible Credit Union
- Use findyourcreditunion.co.uk
- Enter your postcode
- See list of credit unions you can join

Step 2: Check Requirements
- Common bond (live/work in area?)
- Minimum opening deposit (usually £5-10)
- ID requirements (passport/driving licence + proof of address)

Step 3: Apply Online or In-Person
- Online: Complete form, upload ID, pay opening deposit
- In-person: Visit branch with ID and opening deposit
- Time: 10-15 minutes online, 30 minutes in-person

Step 4: Set Up Account
- Receive member number
- Set up online banking (if available)
- Receive debit card (if offered)
- Set up standing orders/direct debits

Step 5: Start Saving or Apply for Loan
- To apply for loan: Usually need to save for 3-12 weeks first
- Loan amount often limited to 3x your savings balance
- Some allow loans to new members (with credit check)


CREDIT UNION LOANS - DETAILS
============================

Loan Types:
- Personal loans (£100 to £15,000+ depending on credit union)
- Debt consolidation loans
- Christmas loans
- Home improvement loans
- Car loans

Interest Rates:
- Legal maximum: 42.6% APR (set by government)
- Typical range: 12.7% to 26.8% APR
- Best rates for those with good credit/savings history
- Rates often lower than credit cards and payday loans

Loan Terms:
- 3 months to 5 years (varies by amount and credit union)
- No hidden fees or charges
- Free life insurance often included (pays off loan if you die)
- Early repayment usually allowed (small fee may apply)

Requirements:
- Common bond eligibility
- Proof of income (payslips, bank statements)
- Credit check (but more flexible than banks)
- Affordability assessment
- Often need to be saving member first (3-12 weeks)

PROS & CONS FOR YOUR SITUATION
===============================

ADVANTAGES:

✅ May offer better loan rates than your current debts
✅ More personal service
✅ Community-focused
✅ FSCS protected (same safety as banks)
✅ Can help build credit history
✅ Free life insurance on loans
✅ Dividends on savings

DISADVANTAGES:

❌ Often require saving before lending

❌ Loan approval not guaranteed
❌ Credit check still required


SHOULD YOU USE A CREDIT UNION?
================================

IMMEDIATE (Phase 1-2):
- Optional: Check eligibility for debt consolidation loan
- If rate < your current debt interest: Consider consolidating
- Otherwise: Continue with debt snowball as planned
- Can join and start small savings (£5-10/month) to build relationship

SHORT-TERM (Phase 3-5):
- Good place for emergency fund after £1,000 reached
- Alternative to keeping all savings in one place
- Higher interest than most banks
- Ethical place to grow wealth

LONG-TERM (Phase 6+):
- Potential source of business funding (ethical lending)
- Established relationship = easier approval
- Alternative to commercial banks for business loans
- Community support for local business
